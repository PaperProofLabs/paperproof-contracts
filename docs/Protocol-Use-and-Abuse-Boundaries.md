# Protocol Use and Abuse Boundaries

This document records responsibility boundaries for use of PaperProof Protocol
contracts and official deployed protocol instances. It is project
documentation, not legal advice.

## Protocol and Labs

PaperProof Protocol refers to the open protocol layer and official deployed
protocol instances. PaperProof Labs refers to the originating team and
maintainer of the official interface, SDKs, reference indexer, documentation,
and brand identity.

PaperProof Protocol is infrastructure. Users and third-party interfaces may
interact with official deployed protocol instances through official apps,
third-party apps, SDKs, indexers, wallets, scripts, agents, or direct chain
transactions. PaperProof Labs is not the author, publisher, adviser,
guarantor, custodian, or endorser of every artifact, comment, vote, proposal,
upload, link, or transaction submitted through those paths.

## Events and Objects Are Facts, Not Endorsements

Protocol events and objects record that a protocol action occurred. They do
not automatically establish truth, legality, quality, peer review, investment
value, safety, reputation, or PaperProof Labs endorsement.

Indexers, applications, analytics systems, and airdrop pipelines should treat
events as evidence to validate and interpret, not as final business
conclusions.

## User and Third-Party Responsibility

Users and third-party operators are responsible for their own content,
transactions, uploads, metadata, comments, votes, proposals, citations,
attestations, rankings, frontends, APIs, moderation policies, and downstream
uses of protocol data.

Third-party interfaces may interoperate with PaperProof Protocol but must not
claim to be operated, endorsed, sponsored, approved, or governed by PaperProof
Labs unless separately authorized.

## Official Interface Non-Display

Official PaperProof interfaces may hide, delist, label, de-rank, refuse to
preview, or stop caching content or links for abuse, safety, copyright,
trademark, privacy, fraud, malware, spam, or policy reasons.

Because Sui and Walrus are decentralized infrastructure, interface non-display
does not guarantee deletion of underlying on-chain records, third-party
records, or decentralized storage content.
