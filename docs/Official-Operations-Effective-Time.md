# Official Operations Effective Time

This document records how official PaperProof Protocol contract operations
distinguish pre-launch mainnet setup activity from formal public operations.
It is project documentation, not legal advice.

## Mainnet Deployment Does Not Equal Public Launch

PaperProof mainnet packages, objects, events, transactions, token contracts,
authority objects, funding movements, upgrade tests, smoke tests, governance
tests, SDK tests, indexer tests, website integration tests, Walrus content
tests, migrations, and demos may exist before the official public launch.

Those pre-launch records may be part of setup, validation, migration, testing,
or operational preparation. They should not automatically be treated as final
public operating policy, final treasury policy, final incentive distribution,
final governance behavior, final authority structure, or final token
allocation.

## Public Launch Effective Time

PaperProof Labs may publish a Public Launch Effective Time through official
channels and deployment records. From that time onward, official operations
should follow the formal records and policies linked by PaperProof Labs.

Formal post-launch records may include:

- official deployment manifest;
- official package and object bindings;
- official admin or upgrade authority records;
- official treasury records;
- official incentive or reward distribution records;
- official governance operation records;
- official website and indexer policy records;
- official announcements and release notes.

## Pre-Launch Activity Examples

Pre-launch activity may include:

- contract deployment and object initialization;
- package upgrade or migration testing;
- token registration and metadata setup;
- official object binding validation;
- event, comment, governance, SDK, and indexer testing;
- Walrus upload, hash, preview, and retrieval testing;
- test funding, consolidation, or authority setup;
- demo transactions and smoke tests;
- operational experiments needed before public launch.

## Post-Launch Expectations

After the Public Launch Effective Time, PaperProof Labs should apply stronger
discipline to official contract-related operations, including:

- updating deployment manifests for material changes;
- documenting authority rotations or migrations;
- distinguishing personal addresses from official addresses;
- recording treasury and incentive movements;
- documenting official-to-personal transfers where relevant;
- publishing program-specific rules for airdrops, grants, bounties, or
  incentives;
- preserving transaction digests and checkpoint references for material
  operations;
- using official channels for official contract announcements.

## No Retroactive Interpretation

The publication of a Public Launch Effective Time does not by itself transform
pre-launch tests, migrations, demos, transfers, funding movements, or
operational experiments into official post-launch policy, final allocation
commitments, reward promises, endorsement, or governance precedent.
