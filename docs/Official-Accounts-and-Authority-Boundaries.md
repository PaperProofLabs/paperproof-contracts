# Official Accounts and Authority Boundaries

This document records intended boundaries for official PaperProof Protocol
contract authority, official operational accounts, and personal or third-party
accounts. It is project documentation, not legal advice.

## Protocol, Labs, and Individuals

PaperProof Protocol refers to the open protocol layer and official deployed
protocol instances. PaperProof Labs refers to the originating team and
maintainer of the official interface, SDKs, reference indexer, documentation,
and brand identity.

Individual contributors, maintainers, operators, developers, researchers, or
community participants may have personal wallets, personal accounts, personal
repositories, personal transactions, personal holdings, and personal opinions.
Those personal activities do not automatically represent PaperProof Labs or
PaperProof Protocol.

## Official Authority Sources

Official PaperProof contract authority should be derived from documented
protocol sources, not from informal assumptions about personal activity.

Relevant official authority sources may include:

- official deployment manifests;
- official package IDs and object IDs;
- official admin, upgrade, treasury, incentive, operations, or governance
  addresses;
- official multisig or governance-controlled objects;
- official release notes and deployment records;
- official repository records;
- official docs and runbooks;
- explicit confirmations through official PaperProof Labs channels.

## Personal Addresses Are Not Official by Default

A wallet address, Sui object, transaction, social account, or GitHub account
associated with an individual contributor is not automatically an official
PaperProof Labs address or action.

Personal transactions should not be interpreted as:

- official protocol upgrades;
- official deployment changes;
- official governance actions;
- official treasury or incentive distributions;
- official token-value statements;
- official endorsement of a third-party interface, artifact, market, address,
  or project;
- official commitment to future support, reward, listing, liquidity, or
  protocol behavior.

## Official Address Categories

Where appropriate, PaperProof Labs may maintain distinct official address
categories, such as:

- admin or upgrade authority;
- treasury;
- incentive or reward distribution;
- operations;
- content publishing;
- demo or test activity;
- governance execution.

An address may be official for one purpose but not for another. Official status
should be interpreted according to the relevant manifest, runbook, governance
record, or official announcement.

## Rotation, Revocation, and Change

PaperProof Labs may rotate, revoke, replace, migrate, or deprecate official
addresses, authority objects, manifests, operational accounts, or authority
paths for security, governance, operational, legal, or upgrade reasons.

Old addresses or previously official objects should not be assumed to remain
authoritative unless current official deployment records or governance state
confirm that status.

## Third-Party Misuse

Third parties must not present personal addresses, personal posts, informal
comments, screenshots, test transactions, demo transactions, or unrelated
wallet activity as official PaperProof Labs action unless confirmed through an
official channel.

Applications, indexers, explorers, analytics systems, and governance tools
should use official manifests and canonical protocol validation rather than
personal-account assumptions when determining official deployment status.

## Recordkeeping

For material official authority changes, PaperProof Labs should preserve
reasonable records, such as:

- affected package, object, address, or authority path;
- change reason;
- transaction digest;
- effective timestamp or checkpoint;
- relevant governance or operator approval;
- manifest update;
- public notice or release note, if any.
